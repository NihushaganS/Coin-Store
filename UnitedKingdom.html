United Kingdom Coins

One Penny :

The UK One penny coin is one hundredth of a pound sterling in currency. It has been in use since 1971 in a variety of formats and features a picture of Queen Elizabeth II on the front side. Over the years, the back side has shown a variety of designs, including a crowned portcullis, a portion of the Royal Shield, and most recently, a creation by Matthew Dent that includes portions of the Royal Shield that, when arranged next to one another, complete the full shield.



Five Pence :

The value of a five pence coin in the UK is one-twentieth of a pound sterling. On the front side of this coin, which has been in circulation since 1968, is a picture of Queen Elizabeth II. Over the years, the back side of the coin has had many designs, including representations of a thistle, a crown, and, most recently, a piece of the Royal Shield designed by Jody Clark. To replace the larger, heavier form that was first released in 1968, the current five pence coin is a smaller, lighter design that was released in 1990.




Fifty Pence :

Half of a pound sterling is represented by the UK fifty pence coin. On the front side of this coin, which has been in circulation since 1969, is a picture of Queen Elizabeth II. Over the years, the back side has featured various memorable designs, including those commemorating the 2012 Olympic Games, Beatrix Potter's 100th birthday, and the 950th anniversary of the Battle of Hastings. The twelve-sided shape of the current fifty pence coin was launched in 2019 to replace the old circular design. On the back, it bears the Royal Coat of Arms.



One Pound :

One pound sterling is represented as one pound in the UK on a coin. From its first introduction in 1983 to replace the one pound notes, it has experienced various design modifications. The latest one pound coin debuted in 2017 and has a 12-sided design with alternate smooth and machined edges to make it more challenging to forge. The back side side of the coin displays various designs depending on the year it was minted, including a representation of the Royal Shield, floral emblems of the UK, and designs honoring historical occasions like the First World War Centenary. The front side of the coin shows a portrait of Queen Elizabeth II.



Two Pounds :

A unit of currency equal to two pounds sterling is the UK two pound coin. A regularly circulated denomination, it was first established in 1998 to mark the 50th anniversary of the end of World War II. The back side of the coin has featured a variety of designs over the years, including memorable designs for milestones like the 200th anniversary of Charles Dickens's birth, the 400th anniversary of the Gunpowder Plot, and the 100th anniversary of the Armistice that ended the World War I. The front side of the coin features a portrait of Queen Elizabeth II. The Royal Navy's involvement in the First World War is depicted in the current design of the two pound coin, which was released in 2015. It was designed by Bruce Rushin.
