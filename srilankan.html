Sri Lankan Coins

One Rupee :

The smallest value coin being used in Sri Lanka is the one rupee coin. It is a tiny, circular coin with a 20 mm diameter and a 1.5 mm thickness. The coin is made of nickel-plated steel and has a sacred bo tree design on one side, which is a typical Sri Lankan Buddhist symbol. The number "1" and the words "Sri Lanka" are written in Sinhala, Tamil, and English on the coin's back. Since 2017, the coin's current design has been in use.



Two Rupee :

Another small value currency in use in Sri Lanka is the two rupee coin. It is a circular coin with a 23 mm diameter and 1.8 mm thickness. The coin is made of brass-plated steel and has a temple image on one side to symbolize the nation's rich cultural heritage. The number "2" and the words "Sri Lanka" are written in Sinhala, Tamil, and English on the coin's back. Since 2017, the coin has been in use in its current form.


In Sri Lanka, a variety of two rupee coins are available.



Five Rupee :

The slightly larger value currency in use in Sri Lanka is the 5 rupee coin. It is a circular coin with a 25 mm diameter and 1.9 mm thickness. The lion, a representation of the nation's old civilization, is featured on one side of the nickel-plated steel coin. The number "5" and the words "Sri Lanka" are written in Sinhala, Tamil, and English on the coin's back. Since 2017, the coin has been in use in its current form.

In Sri Lanka, a variety of five rupee coins are available.



Ten Rupee :

The bigger value currency in use in Sri Lanka is the 10 rupee coin. It is a circular coin with a 27 mm diameter and 2.1 mm thickness. The lotus flower, a Buddhist symbol of purity and enlightenment, is featured on one side of the coin, which is constructed of nickel-plated steel. The number "10" and the words "Sri Lanka" are written in Sinhala, Tamil, and English on the coin's back. Since 2015, the coin has been in use in its present form.

In Sri Lanka, a variety of ten rupee coins are available.
