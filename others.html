UAE 1 dirham :

The 1 dirham coin is a circulating denomination of United Arab Emirates currency, issued by the Central Bank of the UAE. The coin is made of a copper-nickel alloy and has a diameter of 24 millimeters. On the obverse side of the coin, you will find the UAE national emblem, which features a falcon with its wings spread, symbolizing the country's strength and pride. The emblem is surrounded by the name of the country in Arabic script. On the reverse side of the coin, you will find the denomination "1" written in Arabic numerals along with the year of minting. The coin also features a design of crossed swords and a palm tree, which are traditional symbols of the UAE. The 1 dirham coin is commonly used in the UAE for small purchases, such as buying a cup of coffee or a snack. It is worth approximately 0.27 USD as of the current exchange rate.
