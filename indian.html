Indian Coins

One Rupee :

The official currency of India is the Indian rupee, which has a value of one. It is a tiny, circular coin that measures 20mm in diameter and weighs 3.79 grams. The official Indian national emblem, the Lion Capital of Ashoka, appears on the coin's front side. In Hindi script, the words "India" and "Truth alone triumphs" are engraved. The coin's value (1 in Hindi) and the year it was produced are both displayed on the back. The coin appears to be silver in color and is made of ferritic stainless steel. The 1 rupee coin is currently in use and is widely used in India for smaller transactions.



Two Rupee :

A unit of the Indian rupee, the country's legal currency, is the two rupee coin. It is a tiny, circular coin that measures 23 mm in diameter and weighs 4 grams. The lion capital of Ashoka, which is the nation of India's official symbol, can be seen on the front of the coin, along with the Hindi-scripted phrases "India" and "Truth alone triumphs". The coin's value (2 in Hindi) and money printing year are both displayed on the back. Yellowish in color, the coin is made of copper, zinc, and nickel. Currently in use and widely used in India for small transactions is the 2 rupee coin.



Five Rupee :

The Indian rupee currency has a value known as the 5 rupee coin. It is one of the coins that is frequently used for small transactions and is currently in use in India. The 5 rupee coin has a diameter of 23 mm, weighs 6 grams, and is manufactured of ferritic stainless steel. The Lion Capital of Ashoka, which serves as India's flag, is featured on the coin's front side. Both sides of the emblem carry the words "Bharat" and "India." On this side of the coin, the number "5" is clearly displayed as well. The image of the renowned Indian leader's face appears on the coin's back side.



Ten Rupee :

The official currency of India is the Indian rupee, which comes in a value of 10. It is a tiny, circular coin that measures 27mm in diameter and weighs 7.71 grams. Together the words "India" and the Lion Capital of Ashoka, which serves as India's national insignia, are depicted on the coin's front side. The Hindi lettering reading "Truth alone triumphs" is visible. The coin's value (10 in Hindi) and the year it was produced are both displayed on the back. The coin has a yellowish color and is made of a nickel-brass alloy. The 10 rupee coin is currently in use and is frequently used in India for local transactions. It's also accepted.
